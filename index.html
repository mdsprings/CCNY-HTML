Bend the Knee

On March 27, 1814 a battle was fought near an Upper Creek village on a horseshoe-shaped bend near Tallapoosa River. Gen. Andrew Jackson, with an army of Tennessee milita, US regulars, and Creek allies held off Cheif Menawa, and his Upper Creek army to gain control of the land. This final battle was the victory the US needed to win the Creek War of 1813-14. In addition to ceeding more than 21 million acres of land to the US, Andrew Jackson was heralded as a hero and would later further cement his place in history as the 7th President of the United States.
